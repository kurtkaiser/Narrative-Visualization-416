Voting Participation by Race In U.S. Presidential Elections

Elections have consequences, tangible consequences on the lives of all Americans. For nearly forty years, election participation steadily declined, but recently, we have seen a reversal of this trend. One reason for this reverse is the recent surge in states that allow voters to submit their ballots by mail. This change was supported out of necessity, wanting to allow citizens to participate in elections while keeping the community-at-large safe. The U.S. Census reported record-setting voter turnout in many locations as a result of these changes.

The surge in participation occurred throughout the population, present in all the various racial and ethnic groups. Use the dropdown menu to view voting data disaggregated by race.
