Voting Participation by Race In U.S. Presidential Elections

Elections have consequences, tangible consequences on the lives of all American. For nearly forty years election participation had steadily declined. Recently we have seen a reversal of this trend. One reason for this reverse is the recent surge in states that allow voters to submit their ballots by mail. This change was supported out of necessity, wanting to allow citizens to participate in elections while keeping the community at large safe. The U.S. Censor reported record setting voter turnout in many locations as a result of these changes.

The surge in participation happens throughout the population, present in all the various racial and ethnic groups.
